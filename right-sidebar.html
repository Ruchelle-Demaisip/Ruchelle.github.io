Maharlika Investment Fund: Is it an Economic Driver?


photo courtesy of Philippine Star

The Maharlika Investment Fund (MIF) or popularly known as Maharlika Wealth Fund has recently sparked heated discussions and debates around the internet as angry citizens opposed to the said bill concerning that the fund will get seed capital from government banks including the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP)

According to the country’s economic team , the said fund will be generated by the government to invest in an intensive and broad-spectrum of outlets such as foreign currencies, fixed-income instruments, bonds, real estate, and infrastructure projects. It seeks to achieve higher returns than storing billions of financial assets in rich-nation bonds. The fund could increase national wealth, indeed. However, considering that we are a less endowed country with a gross saving rate of 20 percent of gross domestic product (GDP) according to the world bank, will it work?

The Budget Secretary, Amenah Pangandaman shared her buoyancy of the MIF’s prosperity as an investment vehicle and economic driver. Despite the assurance of accountability and transparency, experts are still worried that BSP’s independence would pave the way to politics entering the bank’s works as BSP will be giving 100% of its declared dividends as the start-up capital of the said investment in the estimation of P30-P35 billion annually according to BSP Deputy Governor Francisco Dakila, Jr. in a House hearing on Friday, December 9.

Following widespread criticism and public anger,the lawmakers revised the bill and removed Government Service Insurance System (GSIS) and Social Security System (SSS) as citizens fear that this might jeopardize their savings and put the pensioners at risk. However, GSIS and SSS can still invest in Maharlika depending on their respective board’s decisions. It is known that the GSIS president, Jose Arnulfo "Wick" Veloso, showed optimism that the fund will open industries in the country with high capital expenditures through MIF.


Photo courtesy of CNN Philippines

The House of Representatives recently approved the bill on Thursday, December 15 which earned a solid support of 90% or 282 out of the 312 members of the chamber.



The Speaker, Martin G. Romualdez, the principal author of the bill and the presidential cousin, said that the bill includes strict safeguards and measures to protect the money as their way of addressing the concerns of the people.

“The proposed sovereign wealth fund will help President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. keep the country on the high-growth path. We want to assure the public that the management of the fund will follow best practices and the principles of transparency and accountability,” he said.

According to the country’s economic team, rest assure that MWF will ensure economic growth and enhance long-term value of the fund with appropriate risk limits and ensure profitability.
